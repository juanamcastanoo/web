Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying is bullying that takes place over digital devices like cell phones, computers, and tablets.
Cyberbullying can occur through SMS, Text, and apps, or online in social media, forums, or gaming where people can view, participate in, or share content.
Reference: https://www.stopbullying.gov/cyberbullying/what-is-it

Copyright

Copyright is a type of intellectual property that protects original works of
authorship as soon as they are fixed in a tangible form of expression.
Reference: https://www.copyright.gov/what-is-copyright/

Plagiarism

Plagiarism is the representation of another person's language, thoughts, ideas, or expressions as one's own original work.
Although precise definitions vary depending on the institution, in many countries and cultures plagiarism is considered a violation of academic integrity.
Reference: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plagiarism

Software License

A software license is a document that provides legally binding
guidelines for the use and distribution of software.
Reference: https://www.techtarget.com/searchcio/definition/software-license#:~:text=A%20software%20
license%20is%20a%20document%20that%20provides,more%20copies%20of%20the%20
software%20without%20violating%20copyrights.
